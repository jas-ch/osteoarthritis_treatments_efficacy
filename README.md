# Osteoarthritis Pain & NGF Inhibition

## Abstract
Osteoarthritis (OA) pain is linked to nerve growth at the bone-cartilage interface. Inflammatory cytokines (IL‑1β, visfatin) stimulate chondrocytes to produce nerve growth factor (NGF), leading to sensory nerve ingrowth. Current OA treatments only relieve symptoms; they do not stop disease progression.

In this UCSD bioengineering ressarch, we investigated the mechanisms of OA pain and tested two NGF inhibitors – **tanezumab** (known joint side effects) and **LEVI‑04** (novel neurotrophin-3 inhibitor) – against placebo and physical therapy in a rabbit model.

**Key findings:**  
- IL‑1β induced significantly higher NGF production than visfatin.  
- LEVI‑04 provided pain relief comparable to tanezumab but **without deleterious effects on joint structure**, making it a safer candidate for OA pain management.
